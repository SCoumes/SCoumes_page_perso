Quick presentation

I am currently taking a year long sabatical to work on a string of various reflections aiming to consolidate my general views of "important things". As of now, I am still focusing on the very fundamental stuff, which I believe is required for me to move on to more applied topics later.

I write my reflexions down in a companion document, which is supposed to offer a summary of the views I arrive at. This document is public, for whoever is interested. You can find the last version here . If you want the LaTEX source, the public git repository is here .

This is not a book

I know I am presenting a single monlithic pdf, split into thematical chapters and with formating similar to that of a normal book. But it is not meant to be read linearly, there is no forced unity of subject, and not eveything is approached from the same perspective. In a way, my intent is closer to that of a research diary than of a book.

I have tried to make it easy to choose what to read and I intend to improve that more in the future. In the meantime, please feel free to read what you like as if this were a blog. I have spared you my "completely off topic" reflexions, but different chapters still broach vastly different subjects.

So why is this a single pdf ?

Two answers: LaTEX and printing. I am not entirely satisfied with LaTEX but it is the best way to make documents I know. And LaTEX makes pdfs. I thought about writing pure html or using markdown, but I decided against it. One reason to avoid pure html is my desire to have the final product be printable. Formating might have been an issue if I chose another path, so I decided to stick with LaTEX.

This is a work in progress

Even the last version made available is only a work in progress. My writing notes are visible in purple, and anything I wrote is still succeptible to change in the future if I decide to rework it or if I rethink about what is said.

If you dislike anything you see in my writing then I am interested, please tell me why. I welcome any feedback and I use Crocker's rule on demand, though not by default.

wogian ?

"Wog" or "wogian" is a word I made up to refer to my current reflexions. Sometimes, I use "wogian" as an adjective to add in front of things I want to clarify without looking like I am trying to correct everyone. It's convenient for me, nothing more.
